These two fluxes can be considered in equilibrium. Why?

Planetary albedo

  • Not all shortwave radiation that reaches a planet from sun is absorbed. Some fraction is reflected back to Space.
  • The percentage of short-wave radiation reflected back to Space is called planetary albedo α. α is mainly controlled by cloud cover and cloud properties, the extent of ice and snow.
  • Earth’s planetary albedo is α = 31%

Absorption of short-wave radiation on Earth

  • 22% of the solar radiation is reflected by the atmosphere (clouds, droplets, aerosols) and 9% is reflected by the surface.
  • There is no transmission through the Planet’s interior possible, so short-wave absorption must be 1 - α = 100 - 31% = 69%, of those 20% are absorbed in the atmosphere and 49% at the surface.

Effect of an atmosphere

Earth’s known average surface temperature is +15ºC. Our calculation results in a too cold surface temperature because we have not incorporated the atmosphere i.e. the effects of greenhouse gases.

  • The calculation gives us an idea, that without an atmosphere it would be –19ºC on Earth on average. Thus the effect of the atmosphere (natural greenhouse effect) must be worth +34 K!
  • Side note - Humans have changed greenhouse gas concentrations - we call this enhanced greenhouse effect (which will be discussed in future lectures).

This approach would work for a bare planet or Moon, but is not an accurate representation of Earth’s energy balance.

The ‘Greenhouse Effect’

  • The atmosphere is somewhat like greenhouse glass - transparent to short-wave but opaque to long-wave radiation - the term ‘Greenhouse effect’.
  • The Earth is warmer than it would be without this ‘trapping’ effect of the atmosphere. The long-wave process is due to absorption of L↑ and re-emission due to εa and Ta, not to reflection. Note, a portion of re-emission returns to Earth.

Take home points

  • The planet is using (absorbing) only 69% of the solar radiation received. The planetary albedo is 31%.
  • Without atmosphere it would be –19ºC on Earth on average. The natural greenhouse effect is worth +34 K.
  • The atmosphere is in a radiative deficit and the Planet’s surface shows a radiative surplus. Sensible and latent heat fluxes offset the radiative imbalance, i.e. transport energy from the surface into the atmosphere.
