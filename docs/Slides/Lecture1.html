Heat vs. temperature

  • Heat is the total thermal energy content of a body. Expressed as energy, often also per unit mass or per unit volume of a substance. (i.e. J, or J kg-1 or J m-3) . Remember that:
  • Energy is the ability to do work, and work is done when unit force acts over unit distance: 1 N x 1 m = 1 Joule (J)
  • Temperature is the ability of a body to transfer thermal energy to another body. It is a measure of the random kinetic energy or the average thermal energy of its molecules (in K or ï‚°C).

Conservation of energy and mass

  • One of the most powerful laws used in analyzing organism-environment interaction is this law of conservation.
  • Neither mass nor energy can be created or destroyed by any ordinary means.
  • The application is similar to reconciling your chequing account i.e. you can construct a budget or balance to account for all inflows and outflows of heat and mass.
