Factors affecting LBL thickness

  • The thickness of the LBL depends on the following factors:
  • Wind speed
  • Turbulence level
  • Buoyancy (temperature of leaf minus temperature of
  • the air)
  • Leaf size
  • Sheltering by other leaves
  • Leaf shape, orientation and roughness

Factors affecting LBL thickness

  • Increasing speed and turbulence intensity of the air flow decreases LBL thickness
  • Increasing free convection, by causing the object to become warmer than the air, decreases LBL thickness
  • Increasing size of leaf, bud, etc. increases LBL thickness
  • Increasing sheltering increases LBL thickness
  • Changing shape, orientation and roughness has a variable effect

Take home points

  • LBL is the thin air layer covering all objects within which transport is by conduction or diffusion. Outside it, transport is by forced or free convection.
  • The LBL thickens with distance from leading edge
  • LBL provides resistance (rb) to heat, mass, and momentum transfer, e.g., sensible heat flux proportional to temp difference/rb
  • LBL thickness & resistance determined by wind speed, turbulence, buoyancy, leaf size, sheltering and leaf shape & orientation
  • LBL resistance can be calculated using empirical formulae
  • Used to estimate leaf temperature, transpiration rate, CO2 uptake, heat loss, pollutant deposition
